Expertise

CONTEXT

Expertise is a mobile app that helps anyone, anywhere to instantly contact with certified experts in virtually any field.  The project was given as an assignment for my UX Design Certified course at CareerFoundry. The brief required me to design a responsive web application with a mobile-first approach. 

OBJECTIVE

    The app design with these goals in mind:
  • 01.Provide intuitive, simple navigation for users to connect with an expert in nearly any field.
  • 02.Solve users’ everyday problems that they aren’t familiar with in a fast way.
  • 03.Access to the real-time and face-to-face help of an Expert.

ROLE

    The whole process from the concept to final design with handoff for development was my responsibility.  My roles included: 
  • User research, user survey, user interview and personas
  • User flows, IA and sitemap
  • Sketches and wireframing
  • Prototyping and user testing and qualitative/ quantitative analysis
  • Visual design
    • Tools: Balsamiq, Sketch, Invision. Timeline: March - July 2020

DESIGN PROCESS

I followed the User-Centered Design approach to design the new project. Below you can see the six important phases that structured my design process.

FEATURES AND DESIGN CHALLENGES

Features

  • Lower costs

    • It is an affordable question and answers app. Users have free time to try and pay a few fees for contact with an expert. This saved the users’ time and finances.

  • Flexible time and global availability

    • Insofar as there is sufficient Internet access, users can use the app at their time zone, with no time and region limited. It’s easy and free to use.

  • Interactivity with certified experts

    • Interactivity with certified experts, the user will get directly professional answers and more empathy.

     

Design Challenges

  • Give people a comfortable, intuitive way to connect with an expert online, so they can feel more informed rely on the real example.
  • Motivate more people to use the app to solve their problems and find the answers in their daily life.

INTERVIEWS & SURVEYS

I conducted an online survey and user interviews to better understand the needs, goals, and pain points from my potential users. The survey results help me to collect the participant’s demographics information and their attitudes with app or websites. Based on Interviews, I can got specific suggestions on what features I should have, what features shouldn’t have, as well as their needs and goals. 

PERSONAS

Using the qualitative and quantitative data from survey and interview results, I defined the two target group profiles Anna (Student, 22), Dong (Civil Engineering, 42) to better empathize with my main user groups and prioritize goals according to their needs.

USER JOURNEYS

To ensure that my personas find what they need in app, I created user journeys based on how they would accomplish their goals. 

USER FLOWS

By creating user flows, I could see what needed to be developed so that personas’ needs and goals were satisfied.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Based on the insights gained from the initial content audits, competitor analysis and, card sorts with potential users, I defined the sitemap for Expertise.  

WIREFRAMING & PROTOTYPING

Paper Prototypes

Based on the sitemap, I started sketching low-fidelity wireframes on paper. With paper prototypes, the general structure of the application could easily be tested in usability tests. Without much effort, adjustments could be made before going into the much more costly digital implementation.

Clickable Prototypes

I created wireframes, mid- and high-fidelity prototypes after some paper prototyping adjustment. Also, I used InVison to build clickable prototypes.

USABILITY TESTING

I conducted six usability tests using the moderated remote method via Skype. The sessions lasted around 60 to 90 minutes and consisted of a few general questions, then the six participants to go through the app and accomplished three tasks. User reactions were recording and documented at each stage of the process. After testing, I created an affinity map and rainbow spreadsheet with the test results. Errors were measured using Jakob Nielsen’s scale. The goal of this study is to assess the learnability for new users interacting with the mobile app for the first time. I would like to observe and measure if users understand the app, its value, and how to complete basic initial functions such as sign in/sign up, searching for a doctor, and book an appointment with an expert’s experience. Testers give some feedback on a variety of aspects of the prototype. There are have five main issues. For the usability testing result, please see Rainbow Spreadsheet Link.

IMPROVED SCREENS

Based on the result of user testing, I improved some screens.

VISUAL DESIGN

After multiple iterations and optimizing the UX of Expertise, I applied design principles and developed a responsive grid system before creating a style guide, pattern libraries, and design language system. These make design easier to reuse and standardize and safeguard design’s visual integrity. Properly preparing my files for handoff will save time and energy spent with other developers and designers.
